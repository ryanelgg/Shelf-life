### Spaghetti Aglio e Olio `br1`
*A classic Italian pasta with garlic, olive oil, and red pepper flakes. Simple, fast, and incredibly flavorful.*

**Time:** 20 min · **Servings:** 4 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** dinner, quick, easy, vegetarian, comfort-food

#### Ingredients
- Spaghetti — 1 lb
- Garlic — 6 cloves, thinly sliced
- Olive oil — 1/3 cup
- Red pepper flakes — 1/2 tsp
- Fresh parsley — 1/4 cup, chopped
- Parmesan cheese — 1/4 cup, grated
- Salt — to taste

#### Steps
1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-low heat. Add sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking until garlic is golden (about 2 minutes).
3. Add drained pasta to the skillet and toss with the garlic oil. Add pasta water a splash at a time until silky.
4. Toss with fresh parsley and serve with grated Parmesan.

### Egg Fried Rice `br2`
*The ultimate leftover rice rescue. Savory, satisfying, and ready in minutes with whatever veggies you have on hand.*

**Time:** 10 min · **Servings:** 2 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** dinner, lunch, quick, easy, kid-friendly, meal-prep

#### Ingredients
- Cooked rice — 3 cups (day-old preferred)
- Eggs — 3
- Soy sauce — 2 tbsp
- Sesame oil — 1 tsp
- Green onions — 3, sliced
- Vegetable oil — 2 tbsp
- Frozen peas — 1/2 cup
- Garlic — 2 cloves, minced

#### Steps
1. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking.
2. Scramble eggs in the wok, breaking into small pieces. Push to the side.
3. Add garlic and frozen peas, stir-fry for 30 seconds. Add rice and toss everything together.
4. Pour soy sauce and sesame oil over the rice, tossing constantly for 2–3 minutes until heated through.
5. Top with sliced green onions and serve immediately.

### Honey Garlic Chicken `br6`
*Tender chicken thighs glazed in a sticky, sweet, and savory honey garlic sauce. A crowd-pleaser every single time.*

**Time:** 30 min · **Servings:** 4 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** dinner, easy, kid-friendly, high-protein, meal-prep

#### Ingredients
- Chicken thighs — 2 lbs, boneless skinless
- Honey — 1/3 cup
- Soy sauce — 1/4 cup
- Garlic — 4 cloves, minced
- Rice vinegar — 1 tbsp
- Olive oil — 1 tbsp
- Cornstarch — 1 tbsp
- Green onions — 2, sliced

#### Steps
1. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Sear chicken thighs 5–6 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Remove from pan.
3. In the same pan, whisk together honey, soy sauce, garlic, rice vinegar, and cornstarch with 2 tbsp water.
4. Simmer sauce until thickened, about 2 minutes. Return chicken to the pan and coat in sauce.
5. Garnish with green onions and serve over steamed rice.

### One-Pot Mac & Cheese `br7`
*Creamy, cheesy, and made entirely in one pot. This stovetop mac and cheese beats the boxed stuff every time.*

**Time:** 20 min · **Servings:** 6 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** dinner, lunch, easy, kid-friendly, comfort-food, one-pot, vegetarian

#### Ingredients
- Elbow macaroni — 1 lb
- Milk — 3 cups
- Water — 2 cups
- Sharp cheddar cheese — 2 cups, shredded
- Butter — 2 tbsp
- Salt — 1 tsp
- Mustard powder — 1/2 tsp

#### Steps
1. Combine macaroni, milk, water, butter, and salt in a large pot. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
2. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, stirring frequently, for 10–12 minutes until pasta is tender and liquid is mostly absorbed.

### Pasta Carbonara `br23`
*A Roman classic of silky egg sauce, crispy pancetta, and black pepper clinging to every strand of spaghetti.*

**Time:** 20 min · **Servings:** 4 · **Difficulty:** medium
**Tags:** dinner, comfort-food, medium

#### Ingredients
- Spaghetti — 1 lb
- Pancetta or bacon — 6 oz, diced
- Eggs — 3 whole + 2 yolks
- Pecorino Romano — 1 cup, finely grated
- Black pepper — 2 tsp, freshly cracked
- Garlic — 2 cloves, minced

#### Steps
1. Cook spaghetti in well-salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 1 cup pasta water.

*Layers of ziti pasta, ricotta, marinara, and bubbling mozzarella. A crowd-pleasing baked pasta that feeds a family.*

**Time:** 40 min · **Servings:** 6 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** dinner, easy, comfort-food, kid-friendly, meal-prep

#### Ingredients
- Ziti pasta — 1 lb
- Marinara sauce — 24 oz jar
- Ricotta cheese — 15 oz
- Mozzarella cheese — 2 cups, shredded
- Parmesan cheese — 1/2 cup, grated
- Egg — 1
- Italian seasoning — 1 tsp

#### Steps
1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Cook ziti until just barely al dente. Drain.

### Roasted Cauliflower Tacos `br53`
*Smoky cumin-spiced roasted cauliflower in warm corn tortillas with avocado, pickled onion, and a chipotle lime slaw.*

**Time:** 35 min · **Servings:** 3 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** dinner, lunch, easy, healthy, vegetarian, vegan, dairy-free, gluten-free

#### Ingredients
- Cauliflower — 1 head, cut into florets
- Corn tortillas — 9 small
- Avocado — 1, sliced
- Red cabbage — 2 cups, shredded
- Lime — 2, juiced
- Olive oil — 3 tbsp
- Cumin — 1 tsp
- Smoked paprika — 1 tsp
- Chipotle powder — 1/2 tsp
- Red onion — 1/2, thinly sliced
- Apple cider vinegar — 3 tbsp
- Fresh cilantro — to garnish

#### Steps
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss cauliflower with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, chipotle, and salt. Roast 25–28 minutes until golden.
2. Quick-pickle the onion: combine red onion, apple cider vinegar, a pinch of salt, and a pinch of sugar. Let sit 15 minutes.
3. Toss shredded cabbage with lime juice and a pinch of salt for the slaw.
