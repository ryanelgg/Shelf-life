### Shakshuka `br3`
*Eggs poached in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce. A vibrant, warming dish perfect for any meal of the day.*

**Time:** 25 min · **Servings:** 4 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** breakfast, dinner, easy, vegetarian, healthy, one-pot

#### Ingredients
- Eggs — 6
- Canned crushed tomatoes — 28 oz can
- Onion — 1, diced
- Bell pepper — 1, diced
- Garlic — 3 cloves, minced
- Cumin — 1 tsp
- Paprika — 1 tsp
- Olive oil — 2 tbsp
- Feta cheese — 1/4 cup, crumbled
- Crusty bread — for serving

#### Steps
1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion and bell pepper until soft, about 5 minutes.
2. Add garlic, cumin, and paprika. Cook 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 10 minutes.
4. Make 6 wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each. Cover and cook 5–7 minutes until whites are set.
5. Top with crumbled feta and serve with crusty bread for dipping.

### Classic French Toast `br4`
*Golden, custardy slices of bread with a hint of cinnamon and vanilla. A weekend breakfast classic the whole family loves.*

**Time:** 15 min · **Servings:** 4 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** breakfast, easy, kid-friendly, comfort-food, quick

#### Ingredients
- Thick-cut bread — 8 slices
- Eggs — 4
- Milk — 1/2 cup
- Vanilla extract — 1 tsp
- Cinnamon — 1 tsp
- Butter — 2 tbsp
- Maple syrup — for serving

#### Steps
1. Whisk together eggs, milk, vanilla, and cinnamon in a shallow dish.
2. Melt butter in a skillet or griddle over medium heat.
3. Dip each bread slice into the egg mixture, letting it soak for a few seconds on each side.

5. Serve with maple syrup, fresh berries, or powdered sugar.

### Banana Pancakes `br5`
*Fluffy pancakes naturally sweetened with ripe bananas. A great way to use up those brown bananas on the counter.*

**Time:** 15 min · **Servings:** 4 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** breakfast, easy, kid-friendly, quick, vegetarian

#### Ingredients
- Ripe bananas — 2, mashed
- Eggs — 2
- All-purpose flour — 1 cup
- Milk — 3/4 cup
- Baking powder — 1 tsp
- Butter — 1 tbsp
- Maple syrup — for serving

#### Steps
1. Mash bananas in a bowl. Whisk in eggs and milk until smooth.
2. Add flour and baking powder, stirring until just combined (a few lumps are fine).
3. Heat a buttered skillet over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake.
4. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook 1–2 more minutes.
5. Serve stacked with maple syrup and sliced bananas.

**Time:** 15 min · **Servings:** 2 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** breakfast, quick, easy, healthy, vegetarian, dairy-free, gluten-free, kid-friendly

- Eggs — 2
- Almond milk — 1/4 cup (or any plant milk)

### Chia Pudding with Berries `br60`
*Creamy overnight chia pudding made with coconut milk and topped with fresh berries and maple syrup. Prep in 5 minutes the night before.*

**Time:** 5 min · **Servings:** 2 · **Difficulty:** easy
**Tags:** breakfast, snack, quick, easy, healthy, vegetarian, vegan, dairy-free, gluten-free, meal-prep

#### Ingredients
- Chia seeds — 1/4 cup
- Coconut milk — 1 cup
- Vanilla extract — 1/2 tsp

#### Steps
